I'm a postdoctoral fellow working on mathematical and statistical modelling of infectious disease dynamics. I completed my PhD in Individual Interdisciplinary Studies (Mathematics and Statistics) at the University of Manitoba, co-supervised by Julien Arino and Saman Muthukumarana. My research spans a variety of topics ranging from Bayesian MCMC inference, ODE-based compartmental models, uncertainty quantification, and temporal network analysis, with a focus on translating these methods into public health applications to data science related issues. I also teach mathematics at the undergraduate level and am passionate about making quantitative methods accessible.
