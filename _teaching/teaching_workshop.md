## Role Overview

As a Workshop Instructor, I facilitate interactive problem-solving sessions designed to help students deepen their understanding of mathematical concepts through active engagement, peer collaboration, and guided exploration.

## Workshop Focus

- **Active Learning:** Interactive problem sets that encourage students to discover concepts through exploration
- **Collaborative Problem-Solving:** Working in groups to tackle challenging problems and discuss multiple solution approaches
- **Building Intuition:** Developing conceptual understanding alongside procedural skills
- **Supporting Diverse Learners:** Creating an inclusive environment where students feel comfortable asking questions and taking intellectual risks

## Key Responsibilities

- Design and facilitate workshop activities aligned with course learning objectives
- Guide students through problem-solving processes using questioning and scaffolding techniques
- Foster peer collaboration and knowledge-sharing among students
- Provide constructive feedback on student work and reasoning
- Monitor student progress and identify areas where additional support is needed

## Teaching Philosophy

Workshops are spaces where students learn by doing. The instructor acts as a facilitator who helps students develop mathematical thinking skills, confidence, and resilience in problem-solving, rather than simply transmitting information.
